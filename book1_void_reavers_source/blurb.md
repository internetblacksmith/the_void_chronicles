# Void Reavers — Blurb

## Short (Amazon/storefront — under 200 words)

Captain Zara Vega robs ships for a living. She also fixes them on the way out.

In the lawless outer systems, Zara runs the *Crimson Revenge* with a code most pirates think is suicide: steal the cargo, leave the crew alive. It's earned her enemies among the corporations who want her dead and the pirate crews who think mercy is weakness. When a mutiny halves her crew and a corporate armada mobilises to wipe piracy from the stars, Zara builds an impossible alliance — pirates, free colonies, and corporate defectors united against two hundred warships.

Then something else shows up. Something that treats human fleets like insects and the laws of physics like suggestions.

Humanity's first contact isn't with diplomats. It's with judges. And the verdict is probation — fifty years caged in their own solar system, monitored by alien watchers who will sterilise the sector if the rules are broken.

Now the pirate who made her name breaking rules has to enforce them. And the only thing she has to guide her is a stolen photograph of two people she saved twenty years ago and never saw again.

*Void Reavers* is the first book in The Void Chronicles, a space opera series about becoming worthy of the stars.

## One-liner

A pirate captain steals a photo of someone else's family and carries it for twenty years, and somehow that's the thing the whole book is about.
