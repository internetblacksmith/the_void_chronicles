## Chapter 3 — First Blood (3,312w)

**Plot beats**
- (L3-5) Frame: "Twenty-five years ago..." Flashback chapter
- (L5-19) Zara at 19 aboard the *Fortunate Dream*, a cargo hauler; first friend Tommy Chen; Captain Holbrook surrenders to Morrison's crew
- (L19-33) Morrison boards, murders a pleading crewman (Hansen) in cold blood to demonstrate who's in charge
- (L37-57) Lisa Park is dragged out; Morrison mocks her motherhood, offers to kill her "for working for the corporations"
- (L59-99) Zara intervenes: "A trade. Me for her." Morrison doesn't kill Lisa but *conscripts* Zara instead — she chose to save a stranger
- (L107-115) Aboard the *Blood Harvest*, thrown in a cell for three days
- (L117-173) Pit fight against Kroeger; Zara survives five minutes using sand-in-eyes, biting his forearm; Morrison impressed — "I want to see what you become"
- (L183-213) Training montage (compressed): combat, weapons, tactics, ship ops; studies Morrison's patterns; meets Marcus (kind older pirate, "we all have choices, just not always good ones")
- (L191-199) First raid, first kill — a middle-aged man with a wedding ring, centre mass
- (L215-249) Two years in: Morrison's arrogance leads him into a military ambush disguised as a civilian convoy; *Blood Harvest* destroyed; Marcus pushes Zara into an escape pod and dies of dehydration
- (L251-267) Three days of drifting, salvage rescue, scattering at the nearest port; Zara drinks to Marcus and Lisa Park; commits to being "different, better"

**Character state at chapter end**
Zara at 21: traumatised, orphaned twice over (corporate orphanage, then *Blood Harvest*), has killed men with wedding rings, has survival skills, has a proto-code ("piracy without cruelty"). The chapter's purpose is to manufacture the ch1 Zara's morality in reverse.

**New worldbuilding / tech introduced**
- Titan corporate orphanages (Zara's origin)
- The *Fortunate Dream* (cargo hauler, destroyed), the *Blood Harvest* (Morrison's ship, destroyed)
- Morrison as pedagogical figure
- Pit-fighting culture on pirate ships
- Military disguised-convoy traps (foreshadowing ch5 and ch10)

**Key scenes to preserve (with line refs)**
- L29-31: Hansen's execution. Clean, brutal, one sentence. This is the prose working.
- L59-75: The "me for her" offer — the single load-bearing moment for Zara's entire character arc. Everything she becomes hangs on this.
- L125-167: Kroeger pit fight. Concrete, physical, the biting detail. Keep it.
- L195-199: First kill, described in one sentence. The middle-aged man with the wedding ring. The restraint here is exactly right; don't pad this.

**What's broken in this chapter (pre-flag for structural-issues.md)**
- Lines 183-213 collapse two years into a montage paragraph ("She learned that many of Morrison's crew hadn't chosen this life..."). The review's Lisa Park flashback critique lives here: Morrison's entire influence on Zara is told, not shown, and Lisa Park — the person Zara traded her life for — never appears again. She should.

**Dangling threads opened**
- Lisa Park and her daughter (NEVER paid off; this is a severe structural problem)
- Tommy Chen (vanishes; never seen again)
- Zara's orphanage background on Titan (mentioned again but never developed)

**Dangling threads closed**
- Implicit: why Zara has her code (though the causality is asserted, not earned)

## Chapter 8 — The Quantum Drive Gambit (2,477w)

**Plot beats**
- (L3-11) The derelict *UES Prometheus* — lost 40 years ago; modern military tech grafted onto ancient hull; "quantum signature"
- (L13-29) Boarding team; ancient crew quarters with 40-year-old personal effects (the one genuinely atmospheric passage in the chapter)
- (L31-53) Bridge: ripped out and replaced with quantum computers. Countdown timer: 47:23:16 to activation. Zara reads logs: 17 prior tests, each creating larger quantum instabilities
- (L59-75) Quantum Dynamics Corporation arrives — Director Hamilton leading corporate mercenaries to recover the experiment
- (L73-103) Firefight in corridors; Zara calls out that the experiment is about to cascade; Chen discovers that shutting it down will also cause a cascade — the only option is to *redirect* it
- (L105-163) Chen modifies the quantum equations to aim the fold at empty space 17 light-years away; Hamilton agrees to stand down; both sides work together
- (L165-191) Countdown; the *Prometheus* folds out of existence; the other 17 "quantum wounds" seal via Chen's counter-wave
- (L203-213) Hamilton offers covert future favours in shipping manifests; Zara proposes sending the research to every university in human space

**Character state at chapter end**
Zara: now has a reputation beyond pirate circles (a corporate director owes her personally). Chen Wei: promoted in narrative importance — he's now the book's "quantum guy." Hamilton: spared corporate villain, now a potential ally.

**New worldbuilding / tech introduced (MOST OF THE BOOK'S RULES ARE HERE)**
- Quantum drives as theoretical FTL tech (decades-long corporate arms race, never cracked)
- "Quantum signatures" as sensor-detectable phenomena
- Quantum drives work by "folding space-time"
- Failed tests create "quantum wounds" in local space-time that take days to collapse
- Each wound creates "quantum echoes" that build on each other
- 17 wounds + a cascade = "rip in space-time that doesn't close"
- Counter-wave / quantum disruption field (Chen's invention, reused in ch9 and ch10 "Prometheus Protocol")
- Quantum Dynamics Corporation as a player
- "Jump gates" mentioned once (L149) as the current FTL baseline — *contradicted* by ch1/ch2 "hyperspace" and by ch5/ch7 "hyperspace jumps"

**Key scenes to preserve**
- L25-29: The 40-year-old personal effects on the *Prometheus*. "Photos of families who'd never made it to their new world, children's toys, half-finished letters home." This is the closest the book comes to real atmosphere.
- L155-163: Hamilton's collapse from corporate villain to "do whatever your engineer needs" in seconds — the character turn is weak prose-wise but structurally this is the book's first "humans are contradictions" beat, which the Watchers later echo (ch11)
- L185-191: The fold itself. "Simply gone, taking a small piece of reality with it."

**What's broken**
- "Quantum" = magic word. In this chapter alone: quantum signature, quantum drive, quantum computers, quantum field, quantum echoes, quantum wounds, quantum foam, quantum counter-wave, quantum equations, quantum data. The word is doing too much work.
- Chen's competence curve is too steep — he's a pilot in ch1, a field engineer in ch7, and in ch8 he's decoding alien-level physics in 15 minutes (L89-107)
- The FTL model is internally inconsistent. Ships "jump to hyperspace" freely in every chapter, but "quantum drive" is described as the unbuilt FTL holy grail. Decide which one is real.
- Hamilton makes a major heel turn in one page; his characterisation is a fill-in-the-blanks "morally compromised executive"
- L225-233 is another portentous closing ("something new was being born in the void") — cut

**Dangling threads opened**
- Quantum Dynamics Corporation owes Zara (unresolved but vaguely paid off in ch10 when Hamilton defends her)
- Chen Wei's quantum-physics genius (payoff: ch10 Prometheus Protocol, ch12 Watcher education)
- 17 sealed quantum wounds (payoff: ch12 when Jonas Steele reopens them)
- The research broadcast to universities (never mentioned again)

**Dangling threads closed**
- Partial: the "corporate enemies" thread gains one friendly face (Hamilton)
