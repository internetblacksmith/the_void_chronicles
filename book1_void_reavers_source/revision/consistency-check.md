## Voice Bible Violations

- **ch1:9** — "riding low in the void." Banned filler (voice bible 1.1). Replace with specific visual.
- **ch1:99** — "all bulk and no beauty." Banned filler (voice bible 1.1). Replace with concrete ship description.
- **ch3:75** — "Zara said simply." Banned adverb-as-tone-bearer (1.5). The line is strong enough without it. Cut "simply."
- **ch7:77** — "The age of the soft pirate is over." Banned pattern (1.2). This is dialogue from a dying mutineer — arguably in character, but the construction is still the banned one. Consider rephrasing to something Vargas would actually say.
- **ch10:25** — "Bradford smiled coldly." Banned (1.5). Show the coldness through action or cut the smile.
- **ch11:41** — "Zara said simply." Banned (1.5). Same issue. Cut "simply."
- **ch11:105** — "Zara said simply" + a thesis-statement speech to the Watchers. This is the voice bible's 4.1 violation: "Characters do not state the theme." Zara recites exactly what the author wants the reader to think ("I'm a pirate... I steal, I fight, I survive. But I also protect those who can't protect themselves..."). This is the book's most public-facing moment and it reads like a movie trailer. Rewrite so Zara's answer is oblique — let the Watchers infer contradictions from her actions, not her self-summary.

## Dangling Threads

**Deliberate Book 2 hooks (acceptable):**
- The Watcher liaison device Zara receives (ch12:291) — clearly setup for future role.
- Chen Wei departing for advanced instruction (ch12:262-285) — his return is a natural sequel thread.
- The final Watcher transmission toward the galactic core (ch9:155) — acknowledged as "a report" but not resolved. Works as long-term hook.
- Diana Marsh's exit ("You won't see me again," ch12:365) — clean closure for Book 1.

**Potentially unresolved:**
- **Assassination contract on Vega** (ch4:95): Bradford reveals Hyperion has a standing contract. Bradford later allies with Zara in ch10-11, but the contract is never explicitly cancelled. After Voss dies and Bradford takes command (ch10:165), does the contract still stand? A single line of dialogue in ch11 or ch12 could close this.
- **Commander Harrington** (ch5:210-229): Introduced with some depth, offers the harmonic data, hints at professional respect. Then vanishes. Ch10:85 mentions him secondhand (Elisabeth Chen references him leading the corvette force). He never appears in the battle or aftermath. If he's meant to recur in Book 2, fine — but a reader might expect him at Vesta.

## Character Voice

- **ch2:145-234** — The three Brotherhood captains (Steele, Kowalski, Volkov) on the split screen: distinct. Steele is blunt rage, Kowalski is controlled menace, Volkov is terse military. This scene passes the voice test.
- **ch9:35-48** — Iyer vs. Morse at the Pirate Council: distinct. Iyer is direct and unembarrassed; Morse is dismissive and old-guard. Works.
- **ch11:7-33** — Summit scene (Zara, Bradford, Kozel, Vasquez, Krieger): five speakers, all somewhat interchangeable in register. Bradford has "bitterly" tone but his lines could be Kozel's. Vasquez sounds like a plot-delivery mechanism, not a person. This is the weakest multi-voice scene. Suggestion: give Vasquez a verbal tic (she's a scientist — precision language, hedging, "the data suggests" phrasing). Give Kozel the freight captain's idiom she should have.
- **ch11:75-105** — The Watcher encounter: Zara's thesis speech (line 105) sounds nothing like the terse, pragmatic Zara of ch1-10. She suddenly becomes eloquent and philosophical. This is the biggest voice break in the manuscript.
