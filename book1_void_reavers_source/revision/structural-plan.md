## 2. The four big structural moves

These are the review's top fixes, rendered concrete. Every chapter-level change feeds one of these four.

### Move 1: Plant the Watchers before they arrive

Currently the aliens show up in ch9 with zero setup. Three small seeds fix this:

- **Ch2** — Diana Marsh's data chip contains an unexplained sensor ghost in a Brotherhood ship's log (~80w)
- **Ch5** — TDI's Harrington mentions deep-space sensors picking up odd harmonics near the trojan asteroids (~60w)
- **Ch8** — On the *Prometheus*, Zara finds a forty-year-old crew log entry: *"saw something outside the viewport that moved wrong"* (~80w)

Total: +220w. The ch9 first-contact becomes an answer to questions the reader has been carrying for seven chapters.

### Move 2: Dramatize the transformation

Zara starts ethical in ch1 and ends ethical in ch12. The review's sharpest critique. Fix with two new scenes:

- **Ch3 — Morrison pedagogical scene** (~400w): Morrison takes Zara to watch a captured ship being stripped, names each crew member as they're spaced or not spaced, makes her articulate *why*. She tries to find his "rules"; he reveals there aren't any. This is the scene where she decides to be *less* like him, through specific refusal rather than montage.
- **Ch3 — Lisa Park reunion** (~800w): After escaping Morrison, Zara tracks Lisa down. Lisa is terrified of her (Zara is now a known pirate). Zara leaves money — specific amount, specific cover story — without revealing who she is. Lisa closes the door without recognising her.

This makes Zara's code **earned** — not born from Morrison but from what Lisa's daughter would need her mother to be.

### Move 3: Character differentiation (all chapters)

Jax, Chen, Kira, Rodriguez, the Brotherhood captains, the delegation in ch11 — all currently share one voice. Fix with specific tics enforced consistently (per the voice bible):

- **Jax** — "aye" instead of "yes," cybernetic-eye tic when processing, states exact distances in km
- **Kira** — kisses a charm before firing (plant ch1, pay off ch7/9/10/12), clipped and profane
- **Chen Wei** — ex-military reflex "Sir," parade rest, uses military call-signs by habit
- **Rodriguez** — former miner's register: clipped, profane, practical, embarrassed about ideals
- **Brotherhood captains (ch2)** — Steele interrupting, Kowalski silky, Volkov transactional
- **Delegation (ch11)** — Bradford panicked, Kozel political, Vasquez scientific, Krieger military, Zara pirate-direct

This is pervasive but cheap. Most of it is rewriting existing lines, not adding new ones.

### Move 4: Let the climax land

Ch12 is currently 2,649w handling the book's entire ending: time skip, Brotherhood crisis, shadow-entity breach, Watcher arrival, Rodriguez's rebellion, Chen's departure, Zara's ambassadorship, and the final scene. Every one of those beats needs its own breathing room. Target: 6,500w.

Biggest additions inside ch12:
- **+500w** — a grounded "five years later" opening (no more "five years had passed")
- **+700w** — shadow-entity breach as a full cosmic-horror set piece
- **+400w** — Rodriguez's rebellion against the Watchers as a leadership beat (her moment, not Zara's)
- **+300w** — Chen and Zara's actual goodbye
- **+300w** — the final closing image (see Move 5)

### Move 5: The book's final image

Currently the book ends on: *"The greatest treasure any pirate could claim was a future for their people among the stars."* This cannot survive.

**Proposed replacement:** Zara alone, post-crisis, takes Lisa Park's holo-projector from a personal compartment — the one she's carried since ch3 — and looks at it. Then closes it. Then the ship turns. The book ends on the closed compartment and the turn of the hull.

This pays off the Lisa Park thread, honours the whole transformation-through-specific-debt arc, and ends on two physical actions instead of an abstract noun. **The holo-projector becomes the book's spine.**

*Small ch3 continuity add:* Lisa drops the projector when she's herded off, Zara picks it up. Or Zara asks for it (*"if I die out here, someone should remember your face"*). Either way, Zara carries the projector from ch3 to ch12.

## 3. The decisions I need from you

These are the points where I won't proceed without your call. Most are irreversible once planted.

### Decision A: Diana Marsh's true affiliation

The agent flagged three options:
- **A1 — Watcher agent.** She's been nudging humanity toward a survivable first-contact for years. Reveal in ch12. Biggest payoff, but commits the book's mystery character to a specific answer.
- **A2 — Rogue Hyperion operative protecting her family.** Smaller, more human, keeps aliens mysterious.
- **A3 — Deliberately left ambiguous.** One more ch12 encrypted message, no reveal.

**My recommendation: A1.** The payoff retroactively makes her ch2/ch6/ch10 appearances mean something, and it gives the book a non-human intelligence that's been present the whole time. It also sets up Book 2 naturally (Diana as human-Watcher liaison during probation).

**But this is the kind of decision that locks the book's premise.** If you'd rather keep the aliens fully alien and untouchable, pick A2 or A3.

### Decision B: Rico's fate

The agent wants to promote Rico (ch4's one-scene prop) into ch12 as a Brotherhood crewman who tries and fails to stop Jonas Steele. It's elegant — it mirrors Zara's Lisa Park trade in reverse — but it's also a lot of coordination for a secondary character.

- **B1 — Full promotion.** Rico appears in ch12, dies trying to stop Jonas. Thematic mirror of Zara's ch3 moment. Adds ~200w to ch12.
- **B2 — Light reference.** After the ch12 battle, the Brotherhood manifest is recovered and Rico's name is on it. Offscreen, referenced. Adds ~20w.
- **B3 — Drop entirely.** Rico stays as a ch4 one-off; cut his ch4 subplot to 40 lines (saves ~150w in ch4).

**My recommendation: B2.** Cheapest way to land the thematic echo without forcing another named subplot into an already-loaded ch12.

### Decision C: The "population reduction" demand

In ch11, the Watchers demand population reduction as a probation term. In the current draft the phrase is quietly softened to "voluntary population stabilisation" one paragraph later — the author flinched.

- **C1 — Harsh, unresolved.** Watchers demand population reduction; humans agree to "voluntary stabilisation" without the Watchers rescinding the harder term. The ambiguity of what "voluntary" means hangs over Book 2. Morally charged, uncomfortable, better fiction.
- **C2 — Softened on camera.** The Watchers explicitly rescind the harder term after Zara's "we're contradictions" speech. Cleaner, less dark.

**My recommendation: C1.** The discomfort is the point. Science fiction that makes first contact comfortable is lying. Let the probation terms be genuinely harsh and let humanity live with them.

**But** — this is a tonal call. If you want the book to be more optimistic, go C2.

### Decision D: Zara's physical description

Zara currently has **no physical description** in 12 chapters. The agent flagged this — the book's protagonist is literally invisible.

Plant in ch1 revision and reference consistently:
- Build (wiry/solid/tall/short?)
- One distinctive feature (scar? hair colour or streak? facial feature? tattoo?)
- Voice/register (already implied: low, pragmatic)

**I need your preference.** If you don't have one, I'll default to: **mid-height, wiry, close-cropped dark hair with a grey streak at one temple (scar running under it from the Kroeger pit fight in ch3), weathered hands, grey eyes that go black in low light.** That gives me consistent hooks to reference. But this is *your* protagonist — your call.

### Decision E: Revision order

The agent recommends ch12 first (lock the ending). I propose a slightly different order:

- **Phase 1: Ch1** (small, +320w) — voice calibration. Establish the new voice in the opening chapter before committing it anywhere bigger.
- **Phase 2: Ch12** (biggest, +3,850w) — climax rebuild. Needs the voice dialed in, needs to work structurally before anything after it.
- **Phase 3: Ch3** (+1,500w) — the moral spine. Lisa Park + Morrison scenes.
- **Phase 4: Ch2, Ch4, Ch5** — smaller adjustments, foreshadowing seeds planted.
- **Phase 5: Ch6, Ch7, Ch8** — moderate revisions, more seeds, Anton plants.
- **Phase 6: Ch9, Ch10, Ch11** — the big middle expansions, alien arrival, Battle of Vesta, first-contact negotiation.
- **Phase 7: Consistency re-read** of the whole manuscript. Catch drift.
- **Phase 8: Series bible update** — note that Book 1 now ends with probation *beginning* (Book 2's premise changes).

**Why ch1 first, not ch12 first:** Ch1 is small enough to serve as a voice-calibration sketch without weeks of work. Getting it right teaches me the voice, which makes ch12 easier to write correctly the first time. It also means the file that sets the tone for the whole book (ch1) is locked before anything else drifts.

### Decision F: Jax backstory

Agent noted Jax has zero backstory. I'd push back: he doesn't need a full origin. He needs **specificity** — a cybernetic eye tic, the "aye" pattern, one specific reference to a past ship or captain he lost. That's enough for a first-mate character. A full origin story for Jax pushes the word count up without earning it.

- **F1 — Light specificity only** (my recommendation). Tic + speech + one reference to the past.
- **F2 — Full backstory scene.** One scene across ch4-8 where Jax reveals his past to Zara. +200w.
