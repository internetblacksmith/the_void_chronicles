## 1. Characters

### Primary crew of the *Crimson Revenge*

#### Captain Zara "Bloodhawk" Vega
- **Role**: Captain of the *Crimson Revenge*; by ch12 also Ambassador/liaison to the Watcher Monitoring Council
- **Physical**: "weathered hands" (ch1:L3); no other physical description given in 12 chapters. Apparent age: mid-40s (nineteen in ch3's 25-years-ago flashback → ~44 in main story)
- **First appearance**: ch1:L3
- **Origin**: Titan corporate orphanage (ch1:L27 implied by Kira-parallel; ch3:L13 explicit "escaping the corporate orphanage on Titan"; ch6:L173 "girl from the Titan orphanages")
- **Backstory**: Captured at 19 by Rex "The Butcher" Morrison after offering herself in trade for Lisa Park (ch3:L59-99); trained under Morrison for ~2 years; escaped when Morrison's arrogance walked him into a military ambush (ch3:L215-247); rescued by Marcus (pirate mentor figure, died in escape pod, ch3:L249); began her own piracy at 21 with the code "theft without cruelty"
- **Relationship to others**: Captain to Jax, Kira, Chen, Malik; ally-then-friend to Rodriguez; antagonistic-to-respectful with Bradford; owes Krieger; owes Diana Marsh
- **Distinctive features**: "predatory smile" (ch1:L5); "weathered" (ch1:L3); carries a plasma rifle in combat, a pistol in close quarters
- **Speech patterns**: Dry, clipped, procedural in command voice. Philosophical in internal monologue — but dialogue and internal voice are indistinguishable from Rodriguez's (see review)
- **Killed**: "Mad Dog" Reeves (offscreen, ch1:L47); Marcus "The Knife" Kellerman (ch1:L163); Alexei Volkov (ch2:L337-339); Anton Vasquez (ch7:L59); Sean McKenzie (off-direct-page, implied ch7:L163)
- **Status at end of ch12**: Alive; designated Ambassador Vega / liaison to the Monitoring Council; still captain of the *Crimson Revenge* (now "Probation Enforcement Vessel 001"); has lost Chen Wei to the Watchers' education programme

- **Role**: Pilot in ch1-8; field-engineer in ch7-10; quasi-admiral of Free Colony Alliance fleet in ch10:L103; chosen by the Watchers as first human quantum student in ch12:L181

## 4. Economy

### Credit amounts with chapter:line citations

- **40,000,000 credits** — conservative estimate of the *Stellar Mule*'s rare-earth cargo (ch1:L15)
- **50,000 credits** — bounty the Crimson Brotherhood puts on Zara's head (ch2:L47); Zara calls it insulting
- **5,000,000 credits** — Hyperion Transport Consortium's standing bounty on Zara (ch4:L45)
- **32,000,000 credits** — the *Crimson Revenge*'s effective take from the *Stellar Mule* raid, after the Harbourmaster's 20% cut and expenses (ch2:L21)
- **1,600,000 credits** — individual crew share of the ch1 raid, split 20 ways (ch2:L21)
- **20% cut** — Harbourmaster's standing tax on Deadman's Port transactions (ch2:L15)
- **15% cut** — Rodriguez's transit tax for passage through Solar Wind Brotherhood territory (ch5:L45)
- **100,000 credits** — Gerald Hoffman's offered fee to Zara's "hired pirates" for the Titan bank staged robbery (ch6:L41: "Half now, half when the job's done. Fifty thousand credits" — so 50k down, 50k later)
- **47,000,000 credits** — Zara's Titan heist take, transferred from bank slush funds to her shell account (ch6:L149)
- **800,000,000,000 credits (800 billion)** — Bradford's estimated cost of the Corporate Armada (ch10:L25); supposedly self-amortising in 2 years against annual pirate losses
- **"Enough credits to book passage out of the system"** — what mutineers get when exiled at next port (ch7:L179); rough order of 1-10k credits implied

### Economic texture

- **Shipping lanes are narrow corridors** between star systems; piracy is economically viable because traffic is predictable
- **Rare earth metals from Kepler-442b** are a key high-value cargo (ch1:L15)
- **Kraken-class heavy haulers** are the high-tonnage standard
- **"Years of flying" is the break-even for a single major raid** — ch1:L17 describes 40M as "keep them flying for years"
- **Hyperion Transport Consortium** — dominant cargo shipper (ch1, ch4, ch10)
- **Titan Defense Industries (TDI)** — "one of the largest military contractors" (ch5:L219)
- **Quantum Dynamics Corporation** — quantum-research specialist (ch8, ch10)
- **Titan Mercantile Bank** — insolvent in ch6 due to bad colonial-venture investments
- **Colonial venture economics** as a macro — failed investments in colony worlds crashing banks (ch6:L13)
- **Colonial Wars** — a historical referent; the last time a fleet of the Corporate Armada's size was assembled (ch10:L95)

### Contradictions / holes in the economy
- A single 40M credit cargo "keeps them flying for years" in ch1, but Diana Marsh's bounty (50k) and Hyperion's bounty (5M) seem incompatibly scaled. If 1.6M per crew member is life-changing, a 50k bounty is chump change. The bounty economy is not internally consistent.
- The Titan heist's 47M is handled as pure profit with no redistribution, no Harbourmaster cut, no taxes. It just... funds a shell account.
- 800B for the Armada (ch10) vs. 40M for a major cargo (ch1) puts the Armada at ~20,000× the value of a single major raid. The scale feels right only if corporate economies are ~5 orders of magnitude larger than pirate economies, which is probably the intent but is never explicit.

## 5. Tech rules

### FTL / interstellar travel

- **Hyperspace jumps**: The default FTL mode throughout the book. Ships "jump to hyperspace" routinely (ch1:L215, ch6:L143, ch9:L9, etc.). Unrestricted — characters treat jumps as casual commutes.
- **Jump gates**: Mentioned ONCE (ch8:L149) by Hamilton as "the limitations of jump gates" that a true quantum drive would bypass. This implies hyperspace needs gate infrastructure — but no gates are ever described, and ships freely jump without them elsewhere. **CONTRADICTION.**
- **Quantum drive**: The theoretical "holy grail" FTL (ch8:L17). "Every major corporation and government had been trying to crack the technology for decades without success." It would "fold space-time" and reduce years to days.
- **Solar sails** (ch5): Experimental tech on the *Crimson Revenge*; charged particles from the sun propel them at "speeds that would have torn a standard vessel apart." Works only in-system. Fried permanently in ch5.

### Quantum X (the book's magic word — note all uses)

- **Quantum signature** (ch8:L5, ch9:L67): A sensor-detectable phenomenon emitted by quantum tech
- **Quantum drive**: The FTL tech (see above)
- **Quantum field manipulation** (ch8:L41): What the Prometheus researchers were attempting
- **Quantum field** (ch8, ch9, ch12): The underlying substrate; can be built up, cascaded, disrupted
- **Quantum echoes / quantum wounds** (ch8:L49): Ripples in space-time caused by failed experiments
- **Quantum foam** (ch9:L133): What the alien scout's hull dissolves into under fire
- **Quantum equations / quantum data** (ch8, ch9): The Prometheus research data itself, which can be *broadcast at an alien ship* and somehow paralyses it (ch9:L125-135). **This is the book's largest deus ex machina.**
- **Quantum disruption field** ("Prometheus Protocol", ch10:L109): A weaponised version of Chen's counter-wave; can "create localised disruption" that throws off corporate targeting systems
- **Quantum counter-wave / counter-resonance** (ch8:L133, ch12:L83): Chen's specialty; can close quantum wounds, destabilise other quantum fields, paralyse alien ships
- **Quantum theory / quantum education** (ch11, ch12): What the Watchers offer to teach carefully selected humans
- **Quantum contamination** (ch11:L67): How the Watchers categorise humanity's damage to the "substrate of reality"

### Quantum rules (as stated by the book, reconstructed)

1. Quantum fields exist as a substrate of reality; they can be manipulated to fold space-time.
2. Each unsuccessful manipulation creates a "wound" in space-time. Wounds persist; they can be sealed, but without intervention they collapse slowly.
3. Multiple wounds can resonate and cascade, eventually creating a "rip that doesn't close."
4. A sufficient cascade in one sector can collapse the sector within ~100 years.
5. Broadcasting quantum equations/data at a quantum-based alien vessel paralyses it (ch9). (Why? Never explained.)
6. A human fleet broadcasting a coordinated quantum field can reinforce the boundaries between dimensions and help seal a rift (ch12). (Again, why? Never explained.)
7. The Watchers describe quantum as something that "requires wisdom" to wield safely. Humans "discovered it and immediately weaponised it" (ch11:L91).
8. Shadow-entities from "other dimensions" can breach through quantum rips (ch12).

### Continuity flags in the tech rules
- **Hyperspace vs. quantum drive**: the book treats hyperspace as casual standard tech but quantum drives as the unbuilt holy grail. These cannot both be true unless the distinction is explained.
- **Shields**: The *Crimson Revenge*'s shields are routinely described as standard but the alien scout's weapons bypass them entirely. Shields use unspecified physics.
- **Energy weapons**: "Plasma," "torpedoes" (mixed conventional/nuclear), "point defence lasers," "plasma rifles" (handheld). No real rules.
- **Powered armour**: Volkov's Spetsnaz suit (ch2) has magnetic boots, independent power core, can be manually released. 200 kg mass (ch2:L339).
- **Cybernetics**: Jax (cybernetic eye, ch1:L5), Malik Torres (cybernetic arm, ch2:L21), Kira ("augmented reflexes", ch1:L29). No rules about cost, stigma, or how common cybernetics are.
- **Communications**: Encrypted channels; "all frequencies" broadcasts; long-range transmitters can be "fried" with specific jamming (ch4); routed-through-relays untraceable messages (ch4:L159)

### Hand-waves to flag in revision
- Chen Wei's competence: in ch1 he's a pilot; in ch8 he re-derives quantum physics in 15 minutes. Either expand his characterisation or soften the feats.
- The ch9 "broadcast quantum data at the alien" deus ex machina needs either real setup or a different defeat mechanism
- The ch10 "Prometheus Protocol" becoming a fleet-wide quantum disruption weapon in ~3 weeks of prep time
- Rodriguez's ch9 near-death and ch10 return with a new ship — the book skips the recovery entirely
